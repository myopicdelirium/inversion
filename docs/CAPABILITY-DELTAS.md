# Capability deltas: what the tier-one cases require, against what exists

Derived 2026-07-24 by mapping the case ladder onto the engine as of phase 13. The dossiers define "rich enough"; this document is the engineering side of that definition, updated as phases land.

## Already built and validated

* Exit costs: the snare (phase 8), a grip axis.
* Rehearsals and warnings: the harmless ramp (phase 3), signal without harm.
* Engineered scarcity: the food economy and the seen-famine shadow (phases 1, 13).
* Place and person attachment, grief, cohesion (phases 2, 6).
* Attention capture and its regimes (phases 7, 9, 10).
* Foresight, and knowledge of the world as a configurable axis (phases 11, 13).
* Individual heterogeneity as declared distributions (phase 4); population scale to 10k+ (phase 5); byte-deterministic sweeps (phase 4).

## Missing, in dependency order

1. **The other in the ledger** (phase 14, in progress): an agent's valuation counting its bond target's peril, and assistance physics by which presence helps. Required by every tier-one case; the wager question cannot even be posed without it.
2. **Asymmetric, one-to-many bonds**: charismatic authority is not a partner pair. A single agent as the bond target of many, with bond formation through exposure. Required: Jonestown, Heaven's Gate, Munster.
3. **Socially settable knowledge and expectations**: per-agent (not per-world) knowledge switches, settable by another agent's influence: information control; and belief objects: installed expectations with scheduled payoffs that need not exist (the Xhosa prophecy is exactly an installed false expectation term). Required: all tier one; the Xhosa case is almost nothing but this.
4. **Transmission**: bonds, beliefs, and attention states spreading along social ties. Required: everything above city scale; minimal forms needed even for Jonestown (the concerned-relatives channel).

Weimar-tier additions (economic shock transmission, factional identity) are deliberately not specified yet; tier one first.
